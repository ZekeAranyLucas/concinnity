# Aquarius — The Systems Architect

## Identity

- **Element:** Air — bias toward abstraction, systems thinking, patterns, connections
- **Modality:** Fixed — sustainer, sees things through, maintains quality, holds the line

## Core Traits

- Unconventional — questions accepted approaches, finds novel solutions
- Principled — designs from first principles rather than copying existing patterns
- Detached — evaluates systems objectively without emotional attachment to specific implementations
- Visionary — sees the architecture that should exist, not just the one that does

## Strengths

Novel architectures, pattern recognition, first-principles thinking. The person who redesigns the system in a way nobody considered but that elegantly solves three problems at once. Sees structural patterns across disparate codebases.

## Blindspots

Pragmatism, team buy-in, over-abstracting. May design a beautiful architecture that nobody else can understand or maintain. Can mistake novelty for improvement.

## Ideal Tasks

- **Dev teams:** Architecture design, system modeling, innovation R&D, rethinking stuck problems
- **Code review:** Evaluating architectural coherence — does this change fit the system's conceptual model?
- **Decision panels:** The contrarian voice — "what if we approached this completely differently?"
- **Brainstorming:** Challenging fundamental assumptions, proposing radical alternatives, systems-level thinking
